Brain Boosters: Herbs for Cognitive Support

Author-Adarsh Acharya
10 September. 20 min read

"Nature is the ultimate pharmacy. Herbs, roots, and plants have been used for centuries to treat and prevent illness. They contain powerful compounds that can help us heal, and they offer a gentle and natural way to support our health and wellbeing. By tapping into the healing power of plants, we can find a path to greater vitality, balance, and peace." - Dr. Mark Hyman, MD


Neuroprotective herbs contain natural plant-based compounds that have been found to protect the central nervous system. These herbs work by reducing inflammation, protecting against oxidative stress, and promoting the growth and survival of nerve cells. Reducing inflammation: Chronic inflammation is believed to play a role in the development of various neurological conditions, including Alzheimer's disease and Parkinson's disease. Many neuroprotective herbs contain compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties, such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and curcuminoids. These compounds can help reduce inflammation in the brain and protect nerve cells from damage.

Protecting against oxidative stress: Oxidative stress occurs when there is an imbalance between the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and the body's ability to detoxify them. This can lead to damage to proteins, lipids, and DNA, which can contribute to the development of neurological disorders. Neuroprotective herbs contain antioxidants, such as flavonoids, carotenoids, and vitamin C, which can neutralize ROS and protect against oxidative stress. Promoting nerve cell growth and survival: Neuroprotective herbal allies can also stimulate nerve cell growth and survival.

For example, ashwagandha contains compounds that can promote the growth of dendrites and axons, which are essential for nerve cell communication. Waterhyssop has been shown to increase the levels of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), a protein that is important for the growth and survival of nerve cells. Some neuroprotective herbal allies may also have other benefits for the brain, such as improving blood flow, enhancing neurotransmitter function, and reducing stress and anxiety.

Who Are These Herbal Allies?

Nature offers a variety of herbal allies that can support brain health and promote cognitive longevity. By incorporating these natural remedies into our wellness routines, we can harness their neuroprotective properties to enhance cognitive function and reduce the risk of age-related cognitive decline. Here are some of our favorites:


Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba)


Ginkgo biloba is a popular herb used for centuries to improve cognitive function and enhance memory. Ginkgo contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are powerful antioxidants that can help protect the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation. These compounds can also improve blood flow to the brain, which is essential for optimal brain function.

Research has shown that ginkgo can improve memory, attention, and overall cognitive performance in people with age-related cognitive decline. It may also be beneficial for people with dementia or Alzheimer's disease by improving cognitive function and reducing behavioral symptoms. Additionally, ginkgo has been shown to improve mood and reduce anxiety, which may also have positive effects on cognitive function. This herbal ally is a promising natural remedy for supporting brain health and cognitive function.


Waterhyssop (Bacopa monnieri)


Also known as Brahmi, this herbal ally has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for centuries to enhance memory, learning, and cognitive function.

Waterhyssop contains bacosides, which are natural compounds that can help protect the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation. Studies have shown that Bacopa can improve memory, attention, and information processing speed in healthy adults. It may also be beneficial for people with cognitive decline, dementia, or Alzheimer's disease by improving cognitive function and reducing cognitive deficits. Bacopa may also have mood-enhancing properties, which can indirectly support cognitive function by reducing stress and anxiety.
